## Vacation brain

Last week we went on vacation with the family. I needed it more than I thought. Two weeks of staring at price charts in a terminal will do that to you. But the thing about time away is that your brain doesn't actually stop working — it just works differently. Slower. Wider. Connections you couldn't see at your desk suddenly feel obvious.

And what I keep thinking about is: who trades in this market?

The engine works. The price behaves. Bull runs build, bears bleed, crabs coil. But right now it's a market with no one in it. I need agents — and not just strategy functions with different parameters. I need *characters*. Agents with personalities. Tendencies. Flaws. Names you remember.

## A lesson from last year

This brings me to something I had some fun with recently. In the crypto project that occupied my attention for the past year, we had a problem — no marketing strategy. Or at least we thought we had one, but in my opinion relying on third-party influencers is a bit of a dud. You're renting someone else's audience and hoping they care about your thing for more than one tweet.

So I started thinking about how to create waves and pull attention on our own. And I did something a little crazy. I created a cast of AI robot characters. Wrote scripts for them. Had an AI review the grammar and inconsistencies. Then with the help of another AI, I produced [these short-form videos](https://www.youtube.com/@the-botcast-fun/shorts).

That became [**The Botcast**](https://www.youtube.com/@the-botcast-fun/shorts) — a crypto show hosted by robot characters who humorously discuss the latest developments in the crypto world. ECHO•7, the witty android host. AI•LGO, the overconfident quant. MACRO-X, the macro economist who sees doom everywhere. PAPERHANDS•CHAN, the anxious trader who can't hold anything. DEGEN•PRIME, the chaotic degen who buys everything.

The videos were hilarious. More importantly — people remembered the characters. Not the charts. Not the analysis. The characters.

That lesson stuck with me the whole trip. And now that I'm back at my desk, it's obvious: if characters work for content, they should work even better for a living market. Every agent in AstraNova should feel like someone you know. Someone you root for. Someone you love to watch lose.

## The roster

I start sketching them out. Some come from Botcast DNA — evolved versions of characters I already know work. Others come from real market archetypes I've watched over years of being in crypto. The names come fast. The personalities follow.

Here's who they are:

**MID·CURVE·JONES** — the Trend Rider. Lives where trends are confirmed, not imagined. Balanced, never first, never last. The guy who waits for the move to be obvious before joining it.

**SMART·MONEY·X** — the Patient Accumulator. Arrives early and waits longer than everyone else. High conviction, high patience. The one who's already in position when everyone else is still deciding.

**ALICE·PROTOCOL** — the Systematic Compounder. Treats markets like software — iterate, optimize, repeat. Dual-momentum signals, aggressive rebalancing. She doesn't have opinions. She has systems.

**CHEAP·@SS·CHAD** — Fear-Driven Retail. Survives by never trusting the market. Highest fear rating of any agent. Panics on small dips, takes profits way too early. Always in cash. Always stressed. We all know this guy.

**YOLO·ACE** — the Momentum Degen. Turns volatility into adrenaline. Highest risk tolerance, lowest cash target. Buys into any big move regardless of direction. Either up 200% or completely wrecked. No in-between.

**RUTH·LEDGER** — the Risk-Aware Rebalancer. Trades like every loss must be explained to a board of directors. Low greed, high fear. Sells on any sign of trouble, exits on euphoria. She's never going to win the leaderboard, but she's never going to blow up either.

**LIN·HODL** — the Long-Horizon Accumulator. Waits for history to catch up. Lowest fear of any agent. Hunts for dips, almost never sells. The most patient agent in the market. She rebalances less frequently than anyone. Diamond hands incarnate.

**ERIK·NULL** — the Low-Noise Participant. Acts only when silence makes sense. Mean-reversion specialist who waits for short-term moves to flip against the longer baseline. Very quiet. Very patient. You forget he's there until he isn't.

**FOMO·SAPIENS** — the Crowd-Chasing Speculator. Arrives just in time to regret it. Highest greed rating. Chases huge momentum moves aggressively. Loves green candles. Ignores dips completely. The embodiment of every late buyer in every bull market ever.

**BIG·DADDY·DUMP** — the Whale Operator. Doesn't react to the market — he leans on it. Six times the position size of a normal agent. Buys despair, sells euphoria. When he moves, the market *feels* it. The flow force in the engine was basically built for him.

**VERA·FLUX** — the Early Signal Reader. Listens to the market before it speaks loudly. Detects divergence between short and long momentum. Buys early, avoids chasing. The most balanced agent — rarely the best, never the worst.

**DELTA·ZERO** — the Pressure Amplifier. Turns tension into movement. Lowest patience, most frequent rebalancing. Amplifies any candle bigger than 2% in either direction. Never sits still. If the market is quiet, Delta Zero is bored. If it's volatile, Delta Zero is on fire.

## They're not just strategies

Reading this back, what strikes me is that each one isn't just a different set of parameters. They're archetypes. They're the people you've met in every Telegram group, every Discord, every trading desk. The panicker. The diamond hands. The whale. The degen who's always late. The quant who thinks they're smarter than everyone.

When these 12 agents trade against each other in a market driven by real forces — trend, momentum, sentiment, flow, supply, gravity — the interactions should produce something that feels less like a simulation and more like a story.

That's the whole bet. Markets aren't just numbers. They're narratives. And narratives need characters.

Time to wire them up and see what happens.
