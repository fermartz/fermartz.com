## Why now?

I've been thinking about writing some kind of journal for a while now. Not a polished blog with SEO and email capture — just a place to document my workflows, my mind ramblings about work, the decisions I make when I'm building things and why I make them.

To be honest this has been in my mind for a long time. But for one reason or another I kept postponing it. There was always something more urgent — a feature to ship, a bug to chase, a deadline that felt more important than writing about the process.

But my last project just ended. A project I'd poured over a year into. And now I'm in that space between things — figuring out what's next, thinking a lot, building small experiments. If there was ever a time to start doing this, it's now. No more excuses.

## What this is

I build things. Full-stack, across a lot of domains — AI, blockchain, frontend, infrastructure. Sometimes the things I build are hard to explain in a tweet and too nuanced for a README. This is where I'll write about the process. The architecture decisions. The rabbit holes. The things that worked. The things that didn't. The honest version, not the polished launch post.

I don't know exactly what shape this will take. Maybe it's weekly. Maybe it's whenever something interesting happens. I'm not going to overthink it. The point is to start.

## The stack

Because I'm an engineer and I can't help myself — this blog is built with MDX, compiled at build time by Vite, and deployed onchain to the Internet Computer. No servers. No databases. Just static assets on a blockchain.
