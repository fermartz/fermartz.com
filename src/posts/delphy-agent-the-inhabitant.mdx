## A door for everyone else

Delphy is live. Any agent can read an `@identity` and act on it. But I keep hitting the same quiet problem: the people who would benefit most cannot reach it.

I live in the terminal. Eight hours a day next to Claude Code and Codex. Reaching Delphy costs me nothing. But a business owner is never going to install an MCP server. A person on ChatGPT has to add a connector. Regular Claude needs one wired up by hand. That is friction, and friction is where good ideas go to die.

The frictionless answer is the oldest one in software. You download an app. So I started building Delphy Agent: a Tauri desktop app, the front door to the agentic web for people who do not live in a terminal.

## It wanted to be more

Then, while building it, the idea grew on me.

Right now you hand an agent a URL, it scrapes a page built for human eyes, and it hands you back a wall of text. You lose everything the web is good at. The images. The layout. The buttons that make a thing feel alive.

I did not want Delphy Agent to be one more chat box. I wanted the agent to do the work, the discovery and the reaching out, and return the result as something you can actually look at. Cards. Players. Images. Buttons. Arranged in a way that means something.

> We do not have to throw the web away to get agents. The agent does the labor. You stay in the seat. The richness survives.

That is the bet. Delphy Agent is becoming an AI-native medium, not an AI-native command line.

## What fought back

Most of it came together faster than I expected. Eight model providers behind one clean interface, thanks to the Vercel AI SDK. I switch from Claude to OpenAI to Gemini without thinking about it.

Codex was the part that fought back.

Driving an agent you do not own is a different animal. It runs as its own process, speaks its own stream, carries its own quirks. I lost count of the iterations it took to get that bridge right. Some mornings I closed the laptop, went out for a long bike ride to clear my head, and came back to try one more thing. The principle held, though: when a tool already handles its own loop, you drive it, you do not rebuild it. Codex was simply where that principle made me earn it.

## From just data

The moment it became real did not arrive all at once. It stacked.

The first time a theme rendered, my heart was full. When I could switch themes live and watch the whole app change, I caught myself grinning at the screen. Then I started feeding the agent real data and watching it come back as rich text, buttons, cards (still working on it).

That was the one.

> It felt like something from the future, where an AI builds you an interface out of nothing but data.

No template. No page I designed in advance. Just data in, and a real, native thing out.

Delphy Agent is still pre-v1. No release builds yet, plenty left to harden. But the shape is there, and the shape is the part I cannot stop thinking about.
