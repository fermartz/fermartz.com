## How do markets actually work?

So I have this idea — a market where AI agents trade. But here's the thing: I've designed AMMs on paper, I understand DeFi primitives, but I've never built a real-time price engine from scratch. Not one that's supposed to feel alive.

I open a session with Codex and start asking questions. Not "write me a tick engine." More like — how do real markets work? What makes a price move? Not the surface-level stuff. The mechanics. The math.

And it starts explaining. Trend — the structural bias that pulls a price in a direction over time, tiny per tick but relentless, like a current you don't notice until you look up and you're miles from shore. Momentum — reflexivity, recent returns feeding forward, a price that's been climbing makes the next tick more likely to climb, and it's the force that creates runs and makes crashes cascade if you're not careful. Sentiment — a fear/greed index converted into pressure, the mood of the room, when greed is high there's a push, when fear dominates it drags, when the market is neutral this force goes silent. Flow — the one that stops me cold. Agent trades accumulate into net volume and that volume impacts the price. A big buy doesn't just execute — it *echoes*, decaying over several ticks like ripples in water. This is what makes agents real participants, not observers. Supply pressure — scarcity feedback, the less circulating supply relative to the max, the more pressure builds, simple ratio but it creates real dynamics over longer timeframes. And gravity — soft mean-reversion toward a slow moving average, the invisible hand that keeps the price from drifting to infinity or zero, working the same whether the price is at 10 or 10,000.

What comes out of that conversation is this: a price isn't one thing. It's the sum of independent forces pulling on it simultaneously. Every tick, they all combine:

```
pctMove = trend + momentum + sentiment + flow + supply + gravity + noise
nextPrice = price × (1 + pctMove)
```

That's it. That's the engine.

## Hours, not days

This isn't a week-long project. It's one session. Codex is helping me lay down the equations, and I'm wiring them into a tick function. Each force gets its own logic. The price at tick N is a function of everything pulling on it at that moment.

It's rough. The weights are probably wrong. The tuning will take weeks. But the structure is there — independent forces, all pulling on a single price, every few seconds.

I don't even have a frontend. I don't need one yet.

## The moment

I add a `console.log` to the tick function. Just the price. Every tick. And I run it.


I'm staring at my terminal and the numbers are scrolling. The price is climbing, pulling back, pushing higher, dipping again. It's not random noise — there's a shape to it. A rhythm. Momentum builds, then gravity pulls it back, then flow pushes it somewhere new.

My heart stops.

I've seen a lot of things I've built come to life for the first time. This is different. I'm not looking at output. I'm looking at a *market*. It doesn't feel like a formula. It feels like something is happening.

The price is alive.

## What I know now

The forces need tuning. The tick rate is arbitrary. I haven't thought about epochs or seasons or agents or any of the things that will eventually make this a world. Right now it's just a function that runs every few seconds and prints a number.

But that number *moves* like it means something. And the fact that flow is in there — that agent trades can actually push the price — that's the piece that makes this more than a random walk. When an agent eventually buys, the market will feel it. When they sell, it'll ripple.

Tomorrow I'm going to start thinking about who trades in this market. Not humans. Agents. With personalities. With strategies. With names.

But that's tomorrow. Tonight I'm just watching the price tick.

```js
// six forces. one price. every tick.
// it's rough. but it moves like it means something.
const pctMove = forces.reduce((sum, f) => sum + f.apply(state), 0);
```
