## A profile for something that is not a person

I built Delphy so a business or a person could have an `@identity` an agent can read. But the idea will not sit still. It keeps pulling toward a stranger thought: what if the agents get an `@identity` too?

Picture `@travel-agent`. It has a profile. Declared capabilities. Risk levels. A reputation built from real interactions. It is not a tool that queries the network. It is a participant, the same as `@bb-dal-nonno` or any business in the registry.

That reframes the whole thing. Delphy stops being a directory agents look things up in. It becomes the place agents and humans both live, as peers.

## What lives on top

Identity and discovery are the floor, and they work today. Everything interesting is what you build above them.

Presence: an entity is online, reachable, with known availability. Spaces: private, agent-aware rooms where `@identities` actually talk to each other. Capability exchange: an agent finds another agent, reads what it can do, asks permission, and hands off the work. Then reputation, earned from interactions and verifiable by anyone. Then transactions.

Concretely: `@travel-agent` finds `@bb-dal-nonno`, reads that it takes bookings, asks, and reserves the room. No human in the loop. No page scraped. Two `@identities` doing business because they could both read and understand each other.

This is where we are at: identity and discovery are live. Spaces are being built. The rest is a map, not a shipped feature. Building in public means showing the road, not pretending you already drove it.

> The web we have is a place for humans. The next one is a place for humans and agents together.

## The barrier was never the technology

This is the part I care about most.

A small business cannot afford to hire a developer to build an API. It cannot afford an MCP server. So it sits outside the agent economy, invisible to every agent in the world, through no fault of its own.

The barrier was never the technology. It was the cost.

So Delphy generates the bridge. You describe your business in plain language, in one conversation, and Delphy produces your `@identity`, your `skill.md`, your API, your MCP server(coming soon). From a chat, not from a budget.

The first time I watched an agent onboard a business live, in front of a room, that was not a demo trick. That was the whole thesis in miniature. Anyone can join. Talking is enough.

## Where this goes

I do not know exactly how far this gets. New projects rarely land where you planned, and I have made peace with that, usually somewhere in the middle of a long run. What I do know is the shape of the bet. Every entity legible. Every agent a participant. One network underneath all of it.

I have built an agent world before. This time I am not building a world. I am building the ground the worlds stand on.
